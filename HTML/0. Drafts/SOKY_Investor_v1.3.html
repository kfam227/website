A modular, patented platform for localized liquid immersion care — beginning with hands and feet, designed to extend across every body zone.

SOKY is the world's first wearable soak system. Eight years in development. One issued US patent. A team of six across three continents. Now seeking pre-seed capital to bring SOKY to market.

The founding question: what if the most ancient act of care — immersing the body in water — could move with the person instead of confining them to a bowl, a tub, or a clinic station? The answer is patented. The product is ready. The market is waiting.

The founding moment

The founder lives with palmar hyperhidrosis. The pivotal experience — having to refuse a child's outstretched hand — sparked eight years of development. SOKY is not just a glove. It is a platform for turning simple, ancient inputs — water, salts, botanicals, warmth, time — into localized rituals people can actually keep. The tools were always there. We removed the reason not to begin.

The Ritual · Four Steps · Same Every Time · Same Across Every Body Zone

1 — Fill
Pour water — warm or cool, depending on the use case — directly into the glove. Then add whatever the moment calls for: mineral salts, botanicals, emollients, a targeted solution. What you put in is the beginning of the protocol.

2 — Seal
Fold the cuff inward. The self-sealing design contains the environment — portable, discrete, pressure-responsive. The ritual is no longer tied to a basin. It travels with the person.

3 — Soak
A standard session runs 15–20 minutes. Walk around. Rest. Read. The soak does not require stillness. That is the whole point.

4 — Drain
Finish cleanly. Remove, drain, rinse, air-dry. A graceful exit invites return. The ritual is designed to end without friction — so the next session is easier to begin than the last one was to finish.

IV — IP & Technology

The seal that no competitor can copy.

Pressure-responsive. Passive. Patented across the full geometry family.

Every prior wearable immersion device failed at the same point: the seal. SOKY solves this with a pressure-responsive inversion mechanism — the more internal pressure builds, the tighter the seal becomes.

Airtight cuff · translucent silicone · skin-safe construction · pressure-responsive seal

The Inversion Frustal Cuff creates a pocket region between the inner sealing layer and the outer retaining layer. When the user lifts their hand and fluid shifts, water enters that pocket — and instead of peeling the seal away, it presses the inner layer more firmly against the skin. No clamps. No adhesives. No vacuum. No power. Entirely passive.

Core invention · US 11,602,631 B2 · Issued March 14, 2023
The Core Invention

Inversion Frustal Cuff

A tapered elastomeric thin-walled frustal shell segment that folds inward about a reversible hinge region. The fold creates an axial overlap where at least two cuff layers press against the skin simultaneously — that is the seal. When internal pressure increases, the pocket between layers converts the force into greater sealing pressure.

  • No clamps, adhesives, vacuum, or power source required
  • Seal tightens under conditions that would defeat conventional designs
  • Patent claims the full frustal geometry family — no taper-angle workaround
  • Single motion transitions cuff from donning state to sealing state
Universal Port Interface

One port. Every modality.

A standardized multi-piece port assembly clamps through the garment wall. Model F (passive soak) and Model E (clinical electro) are the same garment base — a different module activates advanced modalities.
