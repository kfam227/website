How It Works

The SOKY Ritual — Five Steps.

Every product. Always this order. The ritual never changes — it's the shared language of portable immersion care.

1
Don
Fold the cuff inward — into itself. Slide the glove on, each finger fully into its sheath.
2
Fill
Hold the seal. Bring the opening under the tap. Fill to your preferred level.
3
Seal
Ball your fist — if the glove swells, you're sealed. The cuff tightens with pressure, not loosens.
4
Soak
15–20 minutes. Walk. Read. Rest. The ritual doesn't ask you to stop — it moves with you.

Drain
Slide off, pour out, rinse, air dry. A clean exit invites return.

How the Seal Works — Inversion Frustal Cuff
Every prior wearable immersion device failed at the same point: when the garment moves, hydrostatic pressure peels the seal away from the skin. SOKY inverts this failure mode entirely. The inversion frustal cuff — a tapered, thin-walled elastomeric shell — folds inward at the wrist or ankle, creating an axial overlap region with at least two cuff layers contacting skin. When internal pressure increases, liquid enters a pocket between layers and presses the inner layer more firmly against the skin. The more pressure builds, the tighter the seal becomes.
Why the Geometry Matters
The cuff is not a rubber band. It is a frustum-like shell — a continuously tapered structure whose cross-sectional area varies along its length. This taper distributes sealing forces over an axial band rather than concentrating them on a single circumferential line. The result: the seal works on wet skin, hairy skin, perspiring skin, and across anatomical asymmetries that would defeat a simple elastic ring.

The pending platform patent expands IP coverage beyond the hand to the full family of frustal geometries — feet, arms, legs, knees, elbows, and pelvic regions — with circular, elliptical, parabolic, and superelliptical cross-section profiles. Each new body zone uses the same inversion sealing principle: fold inward, fill, and let pressure do the work.
Sealing Without Power
The overlap seal maintains integrity without external clamps, straps, adhesives, vacuum evacuation, or electrical power. Sealing pressures on the order of 0.5–2.0 psi provide a substantially fluid-tight seal during ordinary motion — walking, reading, reaching, and daily tasks — for treatment sessions of 15–20 minutes or longer. The pocket/pouch region acts as a dynamic pressure buffer: it expands when fluid loads increase (user lifts their hand) and flattens when loads decrease (user lowers their hand), providing resilient sealing across posture changes.

What can SOKY help with?›
SOKY supports personal care routines for hands and feet — hydration, relaxation, post-exercise recovery, and general skin wellness. It pairs with salts, botanicals, and warm water for portable immersion.
Will SOKY leak?›
When donned correctly — cuff folded inward, fingers fully seated — the patented inversion seal holds firmly. The seal actually tightens with movement.

Yes — that's the whole point. SOKY maintains the seal while you move freely. Read, walk, work, or rest.
