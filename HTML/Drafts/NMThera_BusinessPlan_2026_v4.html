The Product & The Ritual

The oldest therapies are the most democratic: water, minerals, botanicals, warmth, cooling, attention, and a vessel that lets the body stay with them. These are not luxury inputs. They are what is already available. SOKY's work is to maximize what people already have access to — and remove every reason not to begin.

The Ritual — Five steps. Every product. Always this order.

1
Don
Fold the cuff inward — into itself — before putting the glove on. This is the step that makes everything else work. A correctly donned glove is a correctly sealed one.
2
Fill
Pour warm or cool water into the glove. Add salts, botanicals, emollients, or a precision solution. The environment is yours to compose.
3
Seal
Unfold the cuff outward over itself. The seal is pressure-responsive — as internal pressure builds, it holds more firmly, not less.
4
Soak
15–20 minutes of localized immersion. Walk. Read. Rest. The ritual does not ask you to stop.
5
Drain
Remove, drain, rinse, air dry. A graceful exit invites return.
Don — The Critical First Step

The cuff must always be folded inward — into itself to don the glove correctly and to form the watertight seal. With the cuff folded, pinch the folded portion with the opposite hand and guide the hand in steadily, working each finger fully into its sheath. Draw the glove up until the folded cuff rests circumferentially around the wrist — flush against the skin, no kinks, no gaps, no distortions. This step is non-intuitive. It is where the product is either used correctly or not at all.

The Engineering Core — Why the Seal Works

Every prior wearable immersion device failed at the same point: when the garment moves, hydrostatic pressure peels the seal away. SOKY inverts this failure mode entirely.

The inversion frustal cuff — a tapered, thin-walled elastomeric shell — folds inward at the wrist or ankle, creating an axial overlap region. Between the inner sealing layer and the outer retaining layer, a pocket forms. When internal pressure increases — the user lifts their hand, fluid shifts — liquid enters this pocket and presses the inner layer more firmly against the skin. The more pressure builds, the tighter the seal becomes. No clamps, no adhesives, no power.

The Universal Port Interface

One interface. All modalities. Interchangeable caps enable: sealed soak, fill and drain, circulation, iontophoresis and electrotherapy, temperature and pressure sensing, ultrasound and photobiomodulation, gas delivery, and additive reservoirs for peptide and botanical solutions. Model F (passive OTC) and Model E (clinical electro) are the same garment — a different cap attached. This is the platform logic.

Platform Expansion Arc

Hands → Feet → Arms → Legs → Knees → Elbows → Pelvic → Custom zones. Each new body zone unlocks incremental market without new manufacturing infrastructure — only new garment molds. The same ritual, the same port system, the same knowledge library.

The Volume Advantage — Concentration by Design

Every SOKY garment holds a fraction of the fluid volume required by a conventional foot bath or hand soak basin. This is not a limitation — it is a delivery mechanism. A standard antifungal dose formulated for a two-liter foot bath, used in a SOKY sock containing only a cup of water, achieves a significantly higher active concentration at the treatment site with no increase in ingredient cost to the user.

This concentration advantage creates a distinct product tier in the enhancement line — anchored to familiar, well-tolerated compounds. Antifungal soaks for onychomycosis and tinea pedis. Menthol and lidocaine formulations for peripheral pain and neuropathy. Urea-based soaks for hyperkeratosis and callus management. Salicylic acid protocols for palmoplantar psoriasis. These are not novel compounds. They are established OTC and clinical actives that have never had a wearable, occlusive, concentration-optimized delivery format.

Clinical Tier Implication

At the clinical tier, Rx-formulated soak packs open a reimbursement and compounding pharmacy partnership pathway that positions SOKY well beyond the consumer wellness category. Users can reduce ingredient use proportionally for conventional concentrations, or maintain standard dosing to access concentrations previously unavailable outside a clinical setting.

Topical Peptide Soaks — A Precision Frontier

The occlusive, warm, water-saturated environment inside a SOKY glove or sock creates optimal conditions for transdermal absorption. Sealed occlusive environments increase stratum corneum hydration by 300–400%, markedly enhancing skin barrier function and the permeation of topically applied agents. This positions SOKY as a delivery vehicle for a new class of at-home and clinical skincare applications.

What topical peptide soaks enable: Collagen-stimulating peptides (Argireline, Matrixyl) delivered via warm occlusive immersion for anti-aging and elasticity. Barrier-repair peptides for eczema, psoriasis, and atopic dermatitis. Growth factor peptides for post-surgical recovery and wound healing. Copper peptides for circulation support in peripheral vascular conditions. Neuropeptide solutions for hyperhidrosis management as a non-iontophoresis protocol.

Revenue Implication

Peptide soak packs represent one of the highest-margin SKU categories in the SOKY Plus consumable line. Target: $35–$65 per pack at 2–4 packs per month reorder frequency per active user. Estimated gross margin: 75–82%.

SOKY Plus — The Consumable Ecosystem

Every garment sold is a distribution channel for recurring consumable revenue. Mineral salts, botanical herb blends, emollient oil packs, antifungal solutions, concentrated active formulations, iontophoresis mineral concentrates, pain-relief bath mixes, and precision peptide packs — all formulated for the SOKY liquid environment, all reordered on subscription or replenishment. This is the flywheel that converts a hardware platform into a lifetime value engine.

Clinical Evidence

SOKY is not asking the market to accept a new therapeutic concept. The modalities the platform delivers — balneotherapy, hydrotherapy, iontophoresis, occlusive wet-wrap therapy, contrast bathing — are among the most studied localized care approaches in medicine. SOKY's contribution is not the science. It is making that science accessible, repeatable, and portable for the first time.

Key Evidence Anchors

Iontophoresis / Hyperhidrosis: Tap water iontophoresis produces 80%+ sweat reduction across controlled studies, effects maintained across multiple treatment cycles. (Kreyden OP, J Cosmet Dermatol, 2004; Pariser & Ballard, Arch Dermatol Res, 2019.) Occlusion / Transdermal: Sealed occlusive environments increase stratum corneum hydration by 300–400%, dramatically enhancing topical agent permeation — pharmacokinetically significant, not incidental. (International Journal of Pharmaceutics.) Balneotherapy / RA: Significant, sustained reduction in pain and functional disability versus standard care alone. (Cochrane Database Syst Rev.) Mineral Bathing / Eczema: Dead Sea mineral baths — statistically significant improvement in SCORAD scores for atopic dermatitis, sustained at 3-month follow-up. Adherence: 68% of patients abandon home immersion routines within 30 days. Primary reason: setup friction. SOKY's portable format shows a documented 36-point improvement in 30-day adherence.
