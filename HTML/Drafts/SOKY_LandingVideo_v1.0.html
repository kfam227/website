The Ritual

Fill. Seal. Soak. Drain. The same four steps whether you're soaking arthritic hands before bed, resetting after competition, or beginning a practitioner protocol. A wearable environment that moves with the person — not a basin they're confined to.

01 — Fill

Water & whatever the moment calls for.

Salts, botanicals, emollients, a thermal enhancer. Simple inputs, better organized. The enhancer goes in with the water — what you put in is the beginning of the protocol.

02 — Seal

The ritual is no longer tied to a basin.

Fold the cuff inward. Portable, discrete, pressure-responsive — the seal tightens as you move rather than failing. It travels with the person. Move freely.

03 — Soak

15–20 minutes. The soak doesn't ask you to stop.

Walk around. Read. Rest. A standard session runs long enough for genuine immersion without demanding a change in schedule. Stillness is not required.

04 — Drain

A graceful exit invites return.

Remove, drain, rinse, air dry. The ritual is designed to end without friction — so the next session is easier to begin than the last one was to finish.
