VIII — Science & Safety

Built to your standard. Not yours to apologize for.

Every mode carries its safety architecture before it ships.

SOKY is clinically literate and safety-first. The science grounds our restraint as much as our confidence — every claim is bounded by the evidence dossier that precedes it.

Occlusion & Stratum Corneum Hydration

A sealed garment creates immersion + occlusion — increasing stratum corneum hydration 300–400% and enhancing transdermal permeation. Session length and post-soak recovery are engineered around maceration risk thresholds.

"Occlusion increases hydration substantially, enhancing penetration but requiring design-level time limits." — Hadgraft & Lane, Int J Pharm, 2016 [5]

Thermal Governance — Heat as a Pharmacokinetic Dial

Warmth significantly increases transdermal flux. All heat-enabled protocol paths (Model E+) include active temperature sensing and hard engineering cutoffs. Warmth is a clinical feature; uncontrolled heat is a hazard.

~500 Dalton rule governs passive absorption. Active modes (electro, ultrasound) extend beyond this threshold.

Iontophoresis — Built on Demonstrated Precedent

Tap water iontophoresis for hyperhidrosis has multiple RCTs and a Cochrane-reviewed evidence base. US 11,602,631 establishes the wearable iontophoresis mechanism. Current and time limits are engineering requirements — not policy preferences.

"Tap water iontophoresis remains a first-line, evidence-based treatment for palmoplantar hyperhidrosis." — JAAD Guidelines, 2022 [13]

Regulatory Posture — Claims Tied to Evidence

Consumer OTC language stays within safe, non-medical bounds. Pro and clinical modes carry explicit compliance strings, pre-specified thresholds, and evidence dossiers. NM Thera does not overclaim. Science is the basis for restraint as much as confidence.

Single-patient architecture required for clinical, wound-facing, and pelvic applications. Validated disinfection SOPs provided to all Clinical tier partners.
