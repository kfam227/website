How It Works

The SOKY Ritual — Five Steps.

Every product. Always this order. The ritual never changes — it's the shared language of portable immersion care.

1
Don
Fold the cuff inward — into itself. Slide the glove on, each finger fully into its sheath.
2
Fill
Hold the seal. Bring the opening under the tap. Fill to your preferred level.
3
Seal
Ball your fist — if the glove swells, you're sealed. The cuff tightens with pressure.
4
Soak
15–20 minutes. Walk. Read. Rest. The ritual moves with you.
5
Drain
Slide off, pour out, rinse, air dry. A clean exit invites return.
Don — The Critical First Step

The cuff must always be folded inward — into itself to form the watertight seal. This step is non-intuitive. It is where the product is either used correctly or not at all.

The ritual of soaking has existed in every human culture that left records. The Romans, the Japanese, the Turks, the Koreans — they all understood something that modern medicine largely forgot: warm water, time, and attention are among the most powerful therapeutic forces available. SOKY is not an invention. It is a remembering — made portable.

After Your Soak — Care Instructions

Rinse with warm water after each use (mild soap optional). Use the clip hook to hang the glove open for air drying. Once dry, store in the travel bag with mesh panel facing up — or clip the bag to a closet rod to keep it accessible. Do not machine wash or tumble dry. With proper care, SOKY is built for 100+ sessions.

Seven ways into the water.

The Volume Advantage — Concentration by Design

Every SOKY garment holds a fraction of the fluid volume required by a conventional foot bath or hand soak basin. This is not a limitation — it is a delivery mechanism. A standard dose formulated for a two-liter basin, used in a SOKY glove containing only a cup of water, achieves significantly higher active concentration at the treatment site with no increase in ingredient cost.

How the Seal Works — Inversion Frustal Cuff

Every prior wearable immersion device failed when movement created peel forces at the seal. SOKY inverts the problem: internal pressure enters a pocket between cuff layers and pushes the inner layer more firmly against the skin. The more pressure, the tighter the seal. No clamps, no adhesives, no power. Covered by US Patent 11,602,631 B2, valid to 2043.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions answered.

Everything you need to know before your first soak.

What can SOKY help with?›
SOKY supports personal care routines for hands and feet — hydration, relaxation, post-exercise recovery, and general skin wellness. It pairs with salts, botanicals, and warm water for portable immersion.
Will SOKY leak?›
When donned correctly — cuff folded inward, fingers fully seated — the patented inversion seal holds firmly. The seal tightens with movement, not loosens.
Can I walk or use my hands?›
Yes — that's the whole point. SOKY maintains the seal while you move freely. Read, walk, work, or rest.
Can I use salts or additives?›
Absolutely. Epsom salts, essential oils, botanical extracts all work beautifully. SOKY's lower volume concentrates ingredients for better delivery.
How long should I soak?›
15–20 minutes is recommended. Adjust based on your comfort and routine.
Is SOKY latex-free?›
Yes. SOKY is made from medical-grade silicone. Latex-free, BPA-free, and allergy-friendly.
Can I use hot or cold water?›
Yes — SOKY supports both warm and cool soaks. Avoid boiling water. Cool soaks are great for inflammation and recovery.
Is SOKY a medical device?›
The current consumer offering (Model B/F) is OTC personal care — Class I, 510(k) exempt. It supports personal care routines. It is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.
How long does SOKY last?›
With proper care, SOKY is built for 100+ sessions. Medical-grade silicone is inherently durable and resistant to degradation.
Can I use SOKY on my feet?›
Foot SOKY is next on the roadmap — expected within 90 days. Same patented seal technology, adapted for feet.

Prepare for Your First Soak

While you wait, here's what to have ready: a tap or sink for filling, a towel nearby for draining, and 15–20 minutes of time you were going to spend anyway — reading, resting, catching up on something. The ritual doesn't ask you to stop what you're doing. It fits into the time you already have.

Your Five-Step Ritual — Quick Reference

1

Don — Fold the cuff inward, into itself. Slide on. Each finger fully into its sheath.

2

Fill — Hold the seal. Bring the opening under the tap. Fill to your preferred level.

3

Seal — Ball your fist. If the glove swells, you're sealed.

4

Soak — 15–20 minutes. Walk, read, rest. The ritual moves with you.

5

Drain — Slide off, pour out, rinse, air dry. A clean exit invites return.
