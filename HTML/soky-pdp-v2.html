How It Works

The SOKY Ritual — Five Steps.

Every product. Always this order. The ritual never changes — it's the shared language of portable immersion care.

1
Don
Fold the cuff inward — into itself. Apply lotion if desired, grip the fold, and slide the glove on. Each finger fully into its sheath.
2
Fill
Hold the cuff seal intact. Bring the opening under the tap. Fill to your preferred level and temperature.
3
Seal
Ball your fist — if the glove swells, you're sealed. The cuff responds to pressure by holding more firmly, not less.
4
Soak
15–20 minutes of localized immersion. Walk. Read. Rest. The ritual doesn't ask you to stop — it moves with you.
5
Drain
Over a sink, slide off and pour out. Rinse, air dry, pack away. A clean exit invites return.
Don — The Critical First Step

The cuff must always be folded inward — into itself to form the watertight/airtight seal. This step is non-intuitive. It is where the product is either used correctly or not at all. With the cuff folded, pinch and guide your hand in steadily until the opening rests flush around the wrist — no kinks, no gaps.

Enhancement System

Seven ways into the water.

What goes into the water defines the ritual. Same garment. Same port. Same five-step ritual. Every mode unlocks a different outcome.

Heat
Circulation · Muscle Relief
Warm water (38–42°C) increases local blood flow, relaxes muscle tissue, and supports joint mobility. SOKY's sealed environment retains heat significantly longer than an open basin — a 15-minute warm soak in a SOKY glove maintains therapeutic temperature throughout the session without reheating. Ideal for morning stiffness, post-work hand fatigue, and arthritis support routines.
Cool
Inflammation · Recovery
Cold immersion (10–15°C) reduces acute inflammation, numbs pain signals, and accelerates recovery after exercise or injury. SOKY's insulated seal keeps cold water cold — no melting ice diluting the effect. Contrast bathing (alternating warm and cool soaks) is a clinician-recommended modality that SOKY makes practical for the first time at home. Used by athletes, PT patients, and anyone managing repetitive strain.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions answered.

Everything you need to know before your first soak.

What can SOKY help with?›
SOKY supports personal care routines for hands and feet — including hydration, relaxation, post-exercise recovery, and general skin wellness. It pairs with salts, botanicals, and warm water to create a portable immersion experience.
Is SOKY latex-free?›
Yes. SOKY is made from medical-grade silicone. It is latex-free, BPA-free, and allergy-friendly.
Can I use hot or cold water?›
Yes — SOKY supports both warm and cool soaks. Use water at a temperature comfortable to touch. Avoid boiling water. Cool soaks are great for inflammation and post-exercise recovery.
Is SOKY safe for sensitive skin?›
SOKY is made from skin-safe, medical-grade silicone designed for extended contact. If you have specific skin sensitivities, consult your dermatologist before use.
Will SOKY leak?›
When donned correctly — cuff folded inward, fingers fully seated — the patented inversion seal holds firmly. The seal actually tightens with movement. Correct donning is the key step.
Can I use salts or additives?›
Absolutely. Epsom salts, essential oils, botanical extracts, and other soak additives work beautifully. SOKY's lower water volume actually concentrates the active ingredients for more effective delivery.
How long should I soak?›
15–20 minutes is the recommended session length. You can adjust based on your comfort and routine.
Can I walk or use my hands?›
Yes — that's the whole point. SOKY maintains the seal while you move freely. Read, walk, work on your phone, or just rest. The ritual doesn't ask you to stop.
Can I use SOKY on my feet?›

How long does SOKY last?›
With proper care (rinse after use, air dry, store flat), SOKY is built for 100+ sessions. Medical-grade silicone is inherently durable and resistant to degradation.

The current consumer offering (Model B/F) is an OTC personal care product — Class I, 510(k) exempt. It supports personal care routines. It is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.
